In a few words: Designer & systems thinker. Improviser. Tech geek & Bauhaus nerd. Lover of cities & Jane Jacobs. Francophile & flaneur. Maker & eater of all things local, sustainable and organic. Painter, with a soft spot for the Bay Area figurative painters & the Impressionists.

My story...

I grew up in idyllic Marin County, CA, nurtured by my dad -- a writer, teacher and thought-leader about the social, political and cultural impacts of technology -- and my mom -- a hair stylist and costumer.

My first grade teacher misinterpreted my big brown eyed stare and quiet curiosity for shyness, urging my parents to enroll me in a drama class. Little did she expect, I transformed into a musical theater kid, playing little orphan Annie three times. And no one has called me quiet since.

I always loved math and the process of breaking complex problems into logical parts. But I also grew up with an art teacher Grandmother who passed on a love of storytelling and painting.

I continued to straddle the techie/fuzzy divide at Stanford, majoring in Urban Studies with focus in Architecture and Urban Design. My interdisciplinary liberal arts education introduced me to 3 big loves: Jane Jacobs, Improv and Design Thinking.

I worked with Eric Paulos at Intel Research Labs in the summers of 2004 & 2005. Paulos' Urban Atmospheres project brought together my love of cities with the world of technology I grew up in (I had an email address at The WELL from the age of 8 and testified for the EFF when I was 10), enabling me to explore the future of urban computing.

We designed and performed Urban Probes to play with the role of technology in the urban environment and observe how our interventions changed the way people interacted with each other and their surroundings.

I joined Google in May 2006 as a Program Manager in Engineering. I design experiences, communities and environments for grassroots innovation -- from leading a group of volunteers in transforming the testing culture of our engineering organization to building The Garage, a physical space for innovation and collaboration.

I also run a 21st Century Salon to bridge my passion for designing experiences, communities and environments with my love of food.
